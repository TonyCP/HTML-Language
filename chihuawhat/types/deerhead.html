Deer Head (Unofficial Type)

Features:

  • Narrow, deer-like head
  • Long snout
  • Less pronounced eyes
  • Larger body with longer limbs

The deer head Chihuahua resembles the face of a deer, with a long snout, large ears, long legs, and a sloping forehead. Deer head Chihuahuas can have a molera, though it is less likely. Research suggests that deer head Chihuahuas tend to have fewer health problems and are more relaxed. They also weigh more than apple head Chihuahuas with an average weight of 8 to 10 pounds.
