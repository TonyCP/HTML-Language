History

Chihuahuas come in many different colors, patterns, and coats that are merely identical. Weighing no more than 6 pounds, the Chihuahua is most known for its small size, but they have many other interesting characteristics as well. This breed has one of the longest lifespans of any dog breed today, as they are able to live well beyond 20 years of age. Despite size, Chihuahuas are also known for their braveness. They do not back down when confronted by other dogs. Chihuahuas are very loyal, fun, and they possess the attitudes of much larger dogs.


Early Origins of the Chihuahua

The Chihuahua breed originated in acient Mexico and is considered to be one of the earliest breeds to exist in the mid-1800s. Chis are believed to be descendants of the Techichi; a much heavier and larger version of todays Chihuahua that became domesticated by many Mesoamerican civilizations and Native North American tribes, that also considered the breed to be sacred for religious purposes. The Techichi had an average weight of 10 to 20 pounds, but shared the same physical characteristics of the Chihuahua. Unlike the Chihuahua that has both long and short coats, the Techichi only possessed longer coats. To see more about the history of Chihuahuas, please visit History.

Chihuahuas In America

Beppie was first AKC-registered Chihuahua to be recorded in the year 1908. The popularity for this breed was low in the early 1900s. This was due to the fact that most people lived on farms and desired much larger dogs for herding, protecting livestock, or hunting purposes and the size of Chis alone rendered them useless. The Chihuahua began to grow in popularity around the mid-1900s as people began to populate cities living in small houses or apartments. This then led them to desire smaller dogs as suitable household companions. The were first pupular around states bordering Mexico, such as New Mexico, Texas, and Arizona and eventually spread to other states and even countries.
